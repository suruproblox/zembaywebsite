Civil Rights and Liberties Bill of Zembay

Ratified on March 5, 2025

Preamble

We, the citizens of Zembay, in recognition of our shared values of justice, equality, and freedom, do hereby establish this Civil Rights and Liberties Bill to guarantee the protection of individual rights and to ensure that every citizen is treated fairly under the law. This Bill enshrines the fundamental freedoms and protections afforded to all individuals within the borders of Zembay.

Article I: Fundamental Rights

Section 1.1: Right to Equality

All citizens of Zembay are entitled to equal protection under the law. No citizen shall be discriminated against based on their race, color, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, pregnancy, disability, age, medical condition (including cancer-related), ancestry, marital status, citizenship, sexual orientation, or status as a Vietnam-era veteran or special disabled veteran.

All citizens shall have access to the same opportunities, services, and protections, regardless of their background or identity.

Section 1.2: Right to Life, Liberty, and Security

Every citizen has the right to life, liberty, and personal security. No citizen shall be deprived of these rights except through due process of law as prescribed by this Constitution and other national laws.

Section 1.3: Freedom of Expression

Every citizen shall have the right to express their thoughts, opinions, and beliefs freely, whether in speech, writing, or any other form of communication, without fear of government censorship or punishment.

This right shall be subject to reasonable restrictions necessary to protect national security, public order, or the rights of others.

Section 1.4: Right to Privacy

Every citizen has the right to privacy in their personal life, including their home, communications, and possessions. No citizen shall be subject to arbitrary interference or attacks on their privacy, except in cases where such interference is authorized by law for the protection of national security, public safety, or the enforcement of criminal laws.

Section 1.5: Right to Assembly and Association

Citizens shall have the right to assemble peacefully and to form associations, including political parties, trade unions, and other organizations. The exercise of this right shall not be hindered unless it poses a threat to public order or safety, and any restrictions must be proportionate to the threat.

Section 1.6: Right to a Fair Trial

Every citizen has the right to a fair and public trial by an impartial and independent tribunal. Citizens shall be presumed innocent until proven guilty and shall have access to legal representation in criminal proceedings.

Article II: Protection Against Discrimination

Section 2.1: Protection Against Discriminatory Practices

Discrimination, whether by the government, private organizations, or individuals, based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, pregnancy, disability, age, medical condition (including cancer-related), ancestry, marital status, citizenship, sexual orientation, or status as a Vietnam-era veteran or special disabled veteran, is prohibited.

Any action or policy that directly or indirectly results in discrimination against any group or individual shall be subject to legal challenge and may be subject to penalties as outlined in Zembay's legal code.

Section 2.2: Equal Access to Public Services

All citizens are entitled to equal access to public services, including education, healthcare, transportation, and employment, without discrimination. The government shall take measures to prevent discrimination in all public services and ensure that all citizens are treated equally and fairly.

Article III: Economic Rights

Section 3.1: Right to Property

Every citizen has the right to own property, whether land, goods, or intellectual property, and the right to dispose of that property as they see fit. No citizen shall be deprived of their property without just compensation or due process of law.

Section 3.2: Right to Work

All citizens have the right to choose their occupation, work freely, and earn a fair and equal wage for their labor. Employers shall not discriminate against employees based on race, gender, religion, or any other protected characteristic.

Article IV: Political Rights

Section 4.1: Right to Vote

All citizens of Zembay, upon reaching the legal voting age, have the right to vote in national, local, and governmental elections. Citizens shall vote freely and without undue influence, and their votes shall be kept confidential. Non-citizens shall not be permitted to vote or participate in Zembay elections.

Section 4.2: Right to Run for Office

Every citizen, who meets the requirements set forth in the Constitution of Zembay, shall have the right to run for public office and participate in the governance of the nation. The right to hold office shall not be denied to any citizen on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, pregnancy, disability, age, or sexual orientation.

Article V: Right to Education

Section 5.1: Right to Education

Every citizen has the right to free and compulsory primary and secondary education. The government shall ensure that education is accessible to all and free from discrimination.

Article VI: Withdrawal of Citizenship

Section 6.1: Voluntary Withdrawal

Any citizen who wishes to voluntarily renounce their citizenship may do so without coercion. Citizens wishing to withdraw must follow the legal procedures set forth in the Constitution of Zembay and may not face punishment or threats as a result of their decision.

Upon voluntary withdrawal, the individual’s bank account, property, and identification may be legally processed and returned or confiscated as necessary to settle any debts or obligations.

Section 6.2: Involuntary Withdrawal of Citizenship

Citizens who commit serious crimes under the Serious Crimes Act may have their citizenship revoked at the discretion of high-ranking government officials or the council. The government shall ensure due process before the withdrawal of citizenship and shall protect the individual’s legal rights during the proceedings.

Article VII: Enforcement and Remedies

Section 7.1: Remedies for Violations

If any individual’s rights under this Bill are violated, they have the right to seek judicial remedies and compensation through the judicial system established by Zembay. Individuals may file complaints of rights violations to the appropriate governmental authorities, who must investigate and address the matter promptly.

Section 7.2: Protection Against Retaliation

No citizen shall face retaliation for seeking justice or making complaints regarding violations of their rights. Retaliation, including threats, harassment, or harm to any individual asserting their rights, shall be subject to legal consequences.

Article VIII: Amendments

This Civil Rights and Liberties Bill may be amended in accordance with the procedures laid out in the Constitution of Zembay. Amendments to this Bill may not infringe upon the fundamental rights and freedoms guaranteed herein.

Signed and Ratified by:

John J. Moore

President of Zembay

Date: March 5, 2025
