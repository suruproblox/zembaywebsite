The Constitution of Zembay

Ratified on November 20, 2023 | Updated June 30, 2024

ARTICLE I. NAME AND PURPOSE

Section B. PURPOSE

The purpose of this County is to have a united community of like-minded people of all types of forms. All activities of this organization must be directed toward this purpose.

ARTICLE II. CITIZENSHIP

Section A. REQUIREMENTS

  • All legal citizens are automatically eligible to vote for any government official no matter their race, age, gender, belief, and opinions.
  • Any non-citizens are not allowed to vote for anything within Zembay, such as government officials.
  • All citizens are to demonstrate good citizenship.
  • All citizens' decisions and actions shall not be discriminated on the basis of race, color, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, pregnancy, disability, age, medical condition (cancer-related), ancestry, marital status, citizenship, sexual orientation, or status as a Vietnam-era veteran or special disabled veteran.
  • All citizens are required to pay a monthly tax of 0.25 USD or 1 gram of .999 fine silver to the government. Failure to pay such tax will be subject to a debt.

Section B. RIGHTS

All citizens are eligible for human rights, which would be listed in the bill of rights. These rights shall not be taken away based on race, color, national origin, religion, sex, gender identity, pregnancy, disability, age, medical condition, ancestry, marital status, citizenship, sexual orientation, or status as a Vietnam-era veteran or special disabled veteran.

Section C. WITHDRAWAL OF CITIZENSHIP

  • Citizens may have their citizenship withdrawn if they commit any serious crimes under the (Serious Crimes Act) under the discretion of high government officials, including council.
  • Upon withdrawal, any bank accounts or documents shall be documented in the archives.
  • Any debt shall be paid before fully withdrawing.
  • Identification may be seized for storage or shredding at any government office upon withdrawal.
  • Citizens must be given a paper copy of their documents upon request.

ARTICLE III. GOVERNMENT

Section A. GOVERNMENT TITLES

  • President
  • Vice President
  • Secretary
  • Council
  • And any other added by council vote.

Section B. DUTIES

  • The President will chair all meetings of Zembay and will call special meetings as needed. The President must vote only in case of a tie.
  • The Secretary will take, record, and file meeting minutes; produce all official correspondence; and maintain records and report on the financial transactions of Zembay unless confidential.

Section C. REQUIREMENTS FOR RUNNING FOR GOVERNMENT AND HOLDING OFFICE

  • All government officials must be over the age of 15 years old.
  • All government officials must not be over the age of 70 years old.
  • All government officials must be a legal citizen of Zembay.

Section D. ELECTIONS

  • Elections for all government titles shall take place in the first month of the new year.
  • Any citizen may vote for any person excluding themselves.
  • Voters shall not be compensated for their vote.
  • All votes must be submitted 4 days prior to the elections.
  • If 2 people tie in the election for a government title, a follow-up vote will be held within 48 hours.

Section E. REMOVAL FROM GOVERNMENT

  • Officials may be removed from government for failure to perform duties or for violation of any laws and/or voted out by citizens and council.
  • Any government official may resign by submitting a letter of resignation to the President. The President may resign by submitting a letter of resignation to the Vice President.

ARTICLE VIII. BY-LAWS AND AMENDMENTS

Section A. BY-LAWS (FORBIDDEN) - ILLEGAL

Section B. AMENDMENTS

  • Amendments must not be changed unless voted by the council and president.
  • Amendments may be added only if voted by the council.
