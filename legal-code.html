Legal Code of the Micronation of Zembay

Ratified on 3/5/2025

Title 1: General Provisions

Section 1.1: Supremacy of Law

This Legal Code serves as the highest legal authority of Zembay. All citizens, residents, and visitors within the nation's jurisdiction are subject to its authority.

Section 1.2: Definitions

Citizen – Any individual legally recognized as a member of Zembay.

Crime – Any act prohibited under this Legal Code, punishable by fines, community service, probation, or a rehabilitation tax.

Rehabilitation Tax – A penalty imposed in place of imprisonment, requiring the offender to pay 100 ZMB per month for the duration of the sentence.

Fine – A one-time monetary penalty imposed for minor violations.

Community Service – Unpaid work benefiting the public, assigned as an alternative to financial penalties.

Probation – A supervised period during which an offender must follow strict behavioral guidelines instead of serving a full sentence.

Parole – Early release from a rehabilitation tax sentence or community service requirement, granted under good behavior.

Title 2: Criminal Law

Section 2.1: Crimes Against the Nation

Treason – Betraying Zembay, including espionage, aiding foreign enemies, or attempting to overthrow the government.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 5 years OR 500 hours of community service and a fine of 10,000 ZMB.

Sedition – Encouraging rebellion or attempting to incite violence against the government.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 4 years OR 400 hours of community service and a fine of 5,000 ZMB.

Sabotage – Intentionally damaging or disrupting government property or services.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 3 years OR 300 hours of community service, plus restitution for damages.

Section 2.2: Crimes Against Persons

Murder – The intentional killing of another person.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 8 years OR 800 hours of community service, and a fine of 20,000 ZMB, plus restitution to the victim’s family.

Manslaughter – Unintentional killing due to reckless or negligent behavior.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 6 years OR 600 hours of community service and a fine of 10,000 ZMB.

Assault and Battery –

Minor Assault: Rehabilitation Tax for 2 years OR 200 hours of community service and a fine of 2,000 ZMB.

Aggravated Assault (serious injury or use of a weapon): Rehabilitation Tax for 4 years OR 400 hours of community service and a fine of 5,000 ZMB.

Kidnapping – Unlawful detention or transportation of a person against their will.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 6 years OR 600 hours of community service and a fine of 7,500 ZMB.

Harassment and Stalking – Repeated unwanted contact or behavior causing distress.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 2 years OR 200 hours of community service and a fine of 3,000 ZMB.

Section 2.3: Crimes Against Property

Theft –

Petty Theft (< 1,000 ZMB): Fine of 2,000 ZMB OR 100 hours of community service, plus restitution.

Grand Theft (> 1,000 ZMB): Rehabilitation Tax for 4 years OR 400 hours of community service, plus restitution.

Burglary – Unlawful entry into a building with intent to commit a crime.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 5 years OR 500 hours of community service and a fine of 5,000 ZMB.

Fraud – Intentional deception for financial gain.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 4 years OR 400 hours of community service and a fine of 6,000 ZMB, plus restitution.

Title 3: Probation and Parole System

Section 3.1: Probation for Repeat Offenders

Eligibility for Probation: Offenders with three or more convictions for similar offenses within five years are subject to probation instead of standard penalties. Probation may last 1 to 5 years depending on the severity of the offense.

Probation Requirements: The offender must report to a probation officer once per month. The offender must not commit any further crimes during the probation period. The offender may be required to attend rehabilitation programs (e.g., financial literacy, anger management). Travel restrictions may be imposed.

Section 3.2: Parole System for Good Behavior

Eligibility for Parole: Offenders serving a rehabilitation tax sentence of 4 years or longer may apply for parole after serving at least 50% of their sentence.

Parole Conditions: Parolees must still pay 50% of their remaining rehabilitation tax balance. Parolees must perform community service for at least 20 hours per month for the remainder of their original sentence. Any new crime committed while on parole results in immediate reinstatement of the full original sentence plus an additional 2-year rehabilitation tax penalty.

Title 4: Civil Law and Dispute Resolution

Contract Fraud – Deliberate misrepresentation or violation of a legal agreement.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 3 years OR 300 hours of community service and a fine of 5,000 ZMB.

Property Disputes – Unlawful occupation or destruction of property.

Punishment: Restitution and a fine of 3,000 ZMB.

Defamation – Spreading false information with intent to harm another’s reputation.

Punishment: Fine of 4,000 ZMB.

Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR)

Minor civil disputes may be settled via mediation or arbitration, avoiding full legal proceedings.

Title 5: Governance and Public Order

Resisting Arrest – Opposing or evading law enforcement.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 2 years OR 200 hours of community service and a fine of 3,000 ZMB.

Bribery – Offering money or favors to influence officials.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 3 years OR 300 hours of community service and a fine of 5,000 ZMB.

Obstruction of Justice – Interfering with a legal investigation or court proceedings.

Punishment: Rehabilitation Tax for 4 years OR 400 hours of community service and a fine of 6,000 ZMB.

Title 6: Amendments and Revisions

This Legal Code may be amended by the National Assembly with the approval of the President. Amendments must be publicly recorded and accessible to all citizens.
