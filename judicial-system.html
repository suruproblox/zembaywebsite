Judicial and Legal System Act of Zembay

Ratified on March 5, 2025

Title 1: General Provisions

Section 1.1: Purpose

The purpose of this Act is to establish the framework for the judicial and legal system of Zembay. This includes the creation, organization, and functioning of courts, the role of judges and other judicial officers, the legal rights of citizens, and the fair administration of justice in accordance with the Constitution of Zembay.

Section 1.2: Definitions

  • Judiciary – The branch of government responsible for interpreting and applying the law, ensuring justice, and resolving legal disputes.
  • Court – A tribunal established by the state to hear and resolve disputes, interpret laws, and issue rulings.
  • Judge – An appointed or elected official who presides over court proceedings, makes legal decisions, and ensures that justice is served in accordance with the law.
  • Defendant – An individual or entity accused of a crime or subject to a legal dispute.
  • Plaintiff – An individual or entity who brings a legal action or lawsuit against another party.
  • Attorney – A licensed legal professional who represents clients in court and provides legal counsel.
  • Prosecutor – A legal official who represents the state in criminal cases and pursues legal action against individuals accused of crimes.
  • Public Defender – A government-appointed attorney who represents individuals who cannot afford private legal counsel.

Title 2: Structure of the Judicial System

Section 2.1: Court Hierarchy

The judicial system of Zembay shall consist of the following courts, listed in order of their authority:

  • Supreme Court of Zembay – The highest court in the land, which serves as the final court of appeal in all matters of law. It has the power to interpret the Constitution and make decisions that affect the entire nation.
  • Court of Appeals – The second-highest court, responsible for hearing appeals from lower courts and reviewing legal decisions for errors. It ensures that justice is applied uniformly across the nation.
  • District Courts – These are the primary courts for the trial of criminal and civil cases. They handle a wide range of cases, from minor offenses to more significant legal disputes.
  • Specialized Courts – These courts address specific types of cases, such as family law, labor disputes, and environmental law. They may be created by legislative action if needed.

Section 2.2: Jurisdiction

Territorial Jurisdiction – Courts in Zembay shall have jurisdiction over legal matters arising within the geographical boundaries of the nation.

Subject Matter Jurisdiction – Each court shall have jurisdiction over specific types of cases, as defined in this Act and by other relevant laws of Zembay.

Title 3: Appointment and Duties of Judicial Officers

Section 3.1: Appointment of Judges

Judges of the Supreme Court and the Court of Appeals shall be appointed by the Supreme Leader of Zembay, subject to confirmation by the National Assembly. Judges of the District Courts and Specialized Courts shall be appointed by the Supreme Leader, with advice and consent from the National Assembly. Judges shall serve fixed terms of 7 years, with the possibility of reappointment. All judicial appointments will be based on merit, legal qualifications, and demonstrated competence in the legal field.

Section 3.2: Independence of the Judiciary

The judiciary shall be independent from the executive and legislative branches of government. Judges shall not be influenced by external pressures, including political, personal, or economic factors, in their decision-making processes. Judicial decisions must be based on the law, not on popular opinion or political considerations.

Section 3.3: Removal of Judges

Judges may be removed from office for gross misconduct, corruption, or failure to perform their duties. A formal investigation and trial will be conducted to determine the grounds for removal, and removal requires a two-thirds vote from the National Assembly.

Title 4: Legal Rights and Protections

Section 4.1: Right to a Fair Trial

Every individual has the right to a fair and public trial, conducted by an impartial tribunal, without unnecessary delay. Individuals are entitled to legal representation, either by hiring their own attorney or through a public defender provided by the government for those who cannot afford legal counsel.

Section 4.2: Right to Counsel

Every defendant has the right to be informed of the charges against them in a language they understand. Every defendant has the right to consult with an attorney of their choosing. If the defendant cannot afford an attorney, one will be provided by the state.

Section 4.3: Presumption of Innocence

All individuals accused of a crime are presumed innocent until proven guilty beyond a reasonable doubt. The burden of proof rests on the prosecution.

Section 4.4: Right to Appeal

Any individual convicted in a District Court or Specialized Court may appeal the decision to the Court of Appeals. The appeal must be based on legal grounds, such as errors in the application of the law or issues with the trial process.

Title 5: Criminal Law and Procedure

Section 5.1: Criminal Court Procedures

Criminal cases shall be tried by District Courts. Serious criminal offenses may be escalated to higher courts for sentencing. The prosecutor represents the government in criminal cases, while the defendant is entitled to defense counsel. Criminal trials shall be public, unless the court deems it necessary to hold the trial in private for reasons of national security or the protection of individuals involved.

Section 5.2: Sentencing and Penalties

Upon conviction, individuals may face penalties including fines, community service, or imprisonment, subject to the severity of the crime. Imprisonment is to be served with a monthly tax payment of 100 ZMB per month as an alternative to jail time (if applicable), depending on the sentence and the severity of the offense. Probation may be granted for less severe offenses, where offenders are monitored and required to fulfill certain conditions.

Title 6: Civil Law and Procedure

Section 6.1: Civil Courts

Civil cases shall be tried in District Courts, unless otherwise specified by the law. Civil courts handle cases such as disputes over contracts, property, and personal injury.

Section 6.2: Civil Procedure

Civil cases are initiated by the plaintiff filing a complaint. The defendant has the right to respond to the complaint and provide evidence in their defense. Decisions in civil cases are based on a preponderance of evidence—the standard that it is more likely than not that the claim is true.

Title 7: Law Enforcement and Judicial Enforcement

Section 7.1: Enforcement of Court Orders

Judicial officers have the power to enforce court decisions through law enforcement agencies. Court orders, including fines and sentences, must be respected by all citizens of Zembay.

Section 7.2: Legal Precedent

Decisions made by the Supreme Court of Zembay serve as precedent and guide the interpretation of law in lower courts. All courts must adhere to the principles set forth by higher courts unless there is a compelling reason to diverge.

Title 8: Amendments and Revisions

Section 8.1: Amendments

This Act may be amended by the National Assembly, with approval from the Supreme Leader, to adapt to changing needs, societal developments, and emerging legal issues. All amendments must be consistent with the Constitution of Zembay and any other national laws.
