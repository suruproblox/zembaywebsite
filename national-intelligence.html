National Intelligence Agency Act of Zembay

Ratified on: March 5, 2025

Preamble

We, the citizens of Zembay, recognizing the need for a secure and vigilant nation, do hereby establish the National Intelligence Agency (NIA) to safeguard our sovereignty, protect national security, and support the interests of Zembay in the global community. The National Intelligence Agency shall be responsible for gathering, analyzing, and protecting intelligence related to the security and wellbeing of Zembay, its citizens, and its interests.

Article I: Establishment of the National Intelligence Agency

Section 1.1: Name and Structure

The official name of the national intelligence agency shall be the National Intelligence Agency of Zembay (NIA).

The NIA shall be an independent body within the government of Zembay, subject to oversight by the President and the Council of Zembay.

Section 1.2: Mission and Purpose

The NIA shall be responsible for:

  • Gathering intelligence, both domestic and international, relating to national security, defense, and the protection of Zembay’s interests.
  • Analyzing information to identify threats to national security, economic stability, and public safety.
  • Providing timely intelligence reports to the President, Council, and relevant authorities to assist in national decision-making.
  • Preventing espionage, terrorism, and other criminal activities that may pose a threat to the state.
  • Supporting the enforcement of Zembay's laws and aiding law enforcement agencies as needed.
  • Ensuring the protection of Zembay’s citizens’ rights by operating within the bounds of Zembay’s Constitution, laws, and international treaties.

Article II: Governance and Oversight

Section 2.1: Director of the NIA

The NIA shall be led by a Director, who shall be appointed by the President with the approval of the Council of Zembay.

The Director of the NIA shall serve a term of five (5) years, subject to reappointment.

The Director is responsible for overseeing all operations, staffing, and strategic direction of the NIA.

Section 2.2: NIA Council

The NIA shall establish an NIA Council, composed of high-ranking officials from the agency, appointed by the Director.

The Council shall advise the Director on major policy decisions and operations related to national intelligence.

Section 2.3: Oversight and Accountability

The NIA shall be subject to oversight by the President and the Council of Zembay to ensure compliance with national laws and the protection of citizens' rights.

A National Intelligence Oversight Committee (NIOC) shall be established to provide regular reviews of the NIA’s activities, including any surveillance or intelligence-gathering methods, to ensure transparency and accountability.

Article III: Hierarchy of Positions in the NIA

Section 3.1: Hierarchy

The NIA shall follow a clearly defined hierarchy to ensure effective operations and decision-making. The following positions are established:

  • Supreme Director (John J. Moore): The highest-ranking official in the NIA, responsible for strategic direction and overseeing all intelligence operations.
  • Deputy Director (Mea): Assists the Supreme Director with day-to-day operations and supports high-level decision-making.
  • Director of Operations (Moonshine): Oversees intelligence operations, surveillance, and investigations.
  • Director of Analysis (mulchi): Analyzes gathered intelligence and produces intelligence reports for government officials.
  • Director of Security and Counterintelligence (muskrat): Protects the agency’s operations, agents, and classified information.
  • Regional Intelligence Directors (melon): Oversee intelligence operations in specific regions of Zembay.
  • Intelligence Officers (lemeck): Field agents responsible for gathering, analyzing, and reporting intelligence.
  • Support Staff (ozzy “Oswald”): Administrative and logistical personnel ensuring smooth agency operations.

Article IV: Available Positions within the National Intelligence Agency (NIA)

The following positions are available within the National Intelligence Agency (NIA) of Zembay. Individuals interested in applying for these positions must meet the specified qualifications and undergo a thorough background check.

  • Intelligence Analyst: Responsibilities include analyzing gathered intelligence, identifying patterns, and producing detailed reports for government officials. Intelligence Analysts must demonstrate strong analytical skills and proficiency in data processing and pattern recognition.
  • Field Agent: Field Agents are responsible for gathering intelligence from domestic and international sources. They must be prepared to conduct surveillance, interrogations, and other investigative activities as required. Applicants for this position should have strong fieldwork skills and be able to operate under high pressure.
  • Cybersecurity Expert: Cybersecurity Experts protect the NIA's digital infrastructure from cyber threats and ensure that sensitive data is secure. They are responsible for monitoring online threats, performing security audits, and responding to breaches. Applicants must have experience in cybersecurity protocols, coding, and systems analysis.
  • Technical Support Officer: Technical Support Officers assist with the maintenance and operation of the NIA's equipment and technology systems. This includes troubleshooting, setting up technical operations, and providing support for field agents and analysts. Applicants should have expertise in IT support, hardware maintenance, and technology management.

Article V: Powers and Functions

Section 5.1: Intelligence Gathering

The NIA is authorized to gather intelligence through lawful means, including surveillance, human intelligence, and open-source intelligence.

Section 5.2: Cooperation with Law Enforcement

The NIA shall cooperate with Zembay’s law enforcement agencies to prevent and respond to security threats.

Section 5.3: Protection of National Security

The NIA shall play a central role in identifying and mitigating national security threats, including espionage, cyberattacks, and terrorism.

Section 5.4: Emergency Response

In the event of an immediate national security threat, the NIA shall take immediate action to neutralize the threat and report to the President and the Council of Zembay.

Article VI: Limitations and Restrictions

Section 6.1: Protection of Civil Liberties

The NIA is prohibited from conducting activities that infringe upon the rights and liberties of Zembay’s citizens unless authorized by law.

Section 6.2: Prohibition of Domestic Political Involvement

The NIA shall not engage in political activities or support any political party within Zembay.

Section 6.3: Oversight of Intelligence Activities

All intelligence operations must be reported to the National Intelligence Oversight Committee (NIOC) for review.

Article VII: Confidentiality and Classification

Section 7.1: Classification of Information

The NIA shall classify information as Confidential, Secret, or Top Secret based on its sensitivity.

Section 7.2: Protection of Sources and Methods

The NIA is authorized to protect its sources and methods of intelligence gathering.

Article VIII: Funding and Budget

Section 8.1: Budgetary Allocations

The NIA shall submit an annual budget proposal to the Council of Zembay for approval.

Section 8.2: Oversight of Financial Management

The NIA must adhere to financial regulations and is subject to penalties for misuse of funds.

Article IX: Final Provisions

Section 9.1: Repeal of Conflicting Laws

Any conflicting laws will be repealed or amended to align with this Act.

Section 9.2: Amendments

This Act may be amended through a vote by the Council of Zembay and approval by the President.

Signed and Ratified by: John J. Moore, President of Zembay

Date: March 5, 2025
