Government Charter of the Micronation of Zembay

Ratified on N/A

Preamble

We, the people of Zembay, in pursuit of sovereignty, stability, and prosperity, hereby establish this Government Charter to define the structure, responsibilities, and functions of our national government. This charter shall serve as the supreme legal framework guiding the governance of Zembay.

Article 1: Structure of Government

Section 1: Branches of Government

The government of Zembay shall be composed of three branches:

  • Executive Branch – Responsible for leadership, enforcement of laws, and administration.
  • Legislative Branch – Responsible for creating and amending laws.
  • Judicial Branch – Responsible for interpreting laws and ensuring justice.

Each branch shall operate independently to maintain a system of checks and balances.

Article 2: The Executive Branch

Section 1: The Head of State

The Head of State shall be known as the President Zembay (or any preferred title). The Supreme Leader shall serve as the primary representative of the nation, oversee foreign relations, and ensure national security. The President may issue executive orders to implement policies when necessary.

Section 2: The Executive Council

The Supreme Leader shall be supported by an Executive Council, composed of appointed ministers responsible for key government functions, including:

  • Minister of Internal Affairs
  • Minister of Finance
  • Minister of Defense
  • Minister of Foreign Affairs
  • Minister of Industry and Trade
  • Minister of Justice

Ministers shall be appointed by the Supreme Leader and serve at their discretion.

Article 3: The Legislative Branch

Section 1: The National Assembly

Legislative power shall be vested in the National Assembly of Zembay, responsible for drafting, debating, and passing laws. The National Assembly shall be composed of elected or appointed representatives (depending on government type). Laws passed by the National Assembly shall require the approval of the Supreme Leader to be enacted.

Section 2: Legislative Process

Any member of the Assembly may propose a law. Laws shall be debated and voted upon, requiring a majority vote to pass. The Supreme Leader holds veto power but may be overridden by a supermajority vote (if applicable).

Article 4: The Judicial Branch

Section 1: The High Court

The High Court of Zembay shall serve as the highest judicial authority, interpreting the laws and resolving disputes. The High Court shall be composed of appointed judges, serving terms determined by the government.

Section 2: Legal System and Rights

All citizens shall have the right to a fair trial. The judicial system shall operate independently from political influence. Laws shall be interpreted in accordance with the Constitution and this Charter.

Article 5: Citizenship and Rights

Citizenship shall be granted through birthright, naturalization, honorary decree, or signing up for citizenship. Citizens of Zembay shall be guaranteed fundamental rights, including:

  • Freedom of speech, expression, and assembly.
  • Protection against unlawful detention or persecution.
  • The right to participate in governance.

Citizens may renounce or be stripped of citizenship under conditions outlined by law.

Article 6: Amendments and Revisions

Amendments to this Charter may be proposed by the National Assembly or the Supreme Leader. Amendments require approval through a majority vote in the National Assembly and the final consent of the Supreme Leader. Emergency provisions may be enacted under extraordinary circumstances, subject to review.

Article 7: National Symbols and Identity

The official name of the nation shall be the Micronation of Zembay. The national flag, anthem, and emblem shall be designated by the government. The official language(s) shall be determined and recognized within national policies.

Article 8: Dissolution and Succession

In the event of leadership vacancy, succession procedures shall follow the predetermined chain of command. If the government is dissolved, power shall transition according to the constitutional guidelines.

Final Provisions

This Charter shall serve as the foundational legal framework for the governance of Zembay. All citizens and government officials shall uphold its principles in service of the nation.
