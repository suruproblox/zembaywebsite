Citizenship Act of Zembay

Ratified on: March 5, 2025

Title 1: General Provisions

Section 1.1: Purpose

This Act establishes the guidelines and procedures for acquiring, maintaining, and losing citizenship in the Micronation of Zembay. The purpose is to clearly define the rights and responsibilities of citizens, ensure the nation's integrity, and promote active and responsible participation in society.

Section 1.2: Definitions

  • Citizen – Any individual legally recognized as a member of Zembay who holds full rights, responsibilities, and privileges under the law.
  • Naturalization – The legal process through which a foreign national may acquire citizenship in Zembay.
  • Dual Citizenship – The status of an individual who is recognized as a citizen of Zembay while maintaining citizenship in another country.
  • Revocation of Citizenship – The legal process by which an individual's citizenship is withdrawn due to violation of laws, national security concerns, or other disqualifying factors.

Title 2: Acquisition of Citizenship

Section 2.1: Birthright Citizenship

Citizenship by Birth: Any person born on the territory of Zembay, regardless of the nationality of their parents, is granted full Zembayan citizenship.

Citizenship by Descent: A child born outside the territory of Zembay to Zembayan citizens is automatically granted Zembayan citizenship, provided at least one parent is a citizen at the time of the child’s birth.

Section 2.2: Citizenship by Naturalization

Eligibility Requirements

  • Be at least 16 years old.
  • Have resided in Zembay for a continuous period of 5 years.
  • Demonstrate proficiency in the Zembayan official language (if applicable).
  • Pass a background check and an interview to assess the applicant’s knowledge of Zembayan laws, culture, and society.
  • Be of good character, with no serious criminal convictions.

Application Process

Applications for naturalization must be submitted to the Ministry of Immigration. The Ministry will process the application, conduct necessary interviews, and hold a public ceremony for those granted citizenship.

Rights and Duties of Naturalized Citizens

Naturalized citizens enjoy the same rights as those born in Zembay. Naturalized citizens are required to swear an oath of loyalty to Zembay during their citizenship ceremony.

Section 2.3: Citizenship by Marriage

Eligibility Requirements

  • A foreign national who has been married to a Zembayan citizen for at least 3 years may apply for Zembayan citizenship, subject to:
    • A continuous 2-year residency in Zembay.
    • Proof of a stable and bona fide marriage, with documentation.
    • Passing a background check and interview.

Process

The application must be submitted to the Ministry of Immigration. The Ministry will process the application, including interviews and residency verification, before granting citizenship.

Title 3: Rights and Responsibilities of Citizenship

Section 3.1: Rights of Citizens

  • Political Rights: Citizens have the right to vote in national elections, referendums, and participate in governmental decision-making.
  • Economic Rights: Citizens are entitled to own property, engage in business, and freely pursue economic activities within the boundaries of the law.
  • Social Rights: Citizens have the right to access healthcare, education, and social services provided by the state.
  • Legal Rights: Citizens are guaranteed the right to a fair trial, freedom of speech, and protection from unlawful detention.

Section 3.2: Responsibilities of Citizens

  • Obedience to the Law: Citizens must adhere to the laws, regulations, and policies of Zembay.
  • Payment of Taxes: Citizens are responsible for contributing to the economic stability of Zembay by paying taxes as required by law.
  • Civic Participation: Citizens are encouraged to participate in civic life, whether through voting, volunteering, or contributing to the nation’s well-being.
  • National Defense: In times of national emergency, citizens may be required to contribute to the defense of Zembay.

Title 4: Loss of Citizenship

Section 4.1: Voluntary Renunciation

A citizen may voluntarily renounce their citizenship by submitting a written declaration to the Ministry of Immigration.

Conditions of Renunciation

  • The individual must not owe any debts, taxes, or fines to the government.
  • The individual must have established citizenship in another country or a valid reason for renunciation.

Section 4.2: Revocation of Citizenship

Grounds for Revocation

  • Conviction of serious crimes, such as treason, terrorism, or acts of espionage.
  • Violation of national security or involvement in activities deemed harmful to the interests of Zembay.
  • Fraudulent acquisition of citizenship, including falsification of documents or misrepresentation.

Process of Revocation

The government may initiate a revocation process by submitting a petition to the Supreme Court. A hearing will be held to determine the validity of the petition and allow the individual to defend their case. If the court rules in favor of revocation, the individual will lose all rights associated with citizenship, including the right to reside and work in Zembay.

Title 5: Dual Citizenship

Recognition of Dual Citizenship

Zembay recognizes dual citizenship. Citizens are allowed to hold citizenship in Zembay while maintaining citizenship in another country, subject to the following restrictions:

  • National loyalty to Zembay must not be in conflict with the duties owed to another country.
  • Citizens must comply with the laws of both countries.

Loss of Dual Citizenship

If a citizen voluntarily or involuntarily acquires citizenship in another country that does not recognize dual citizenship, they may be required to choose between their Zembayan citizenship and the other country’s citizenship.

Title 6: Amendments and Revisions

This Citizenship Act may be amended by the National Assembly, with approval from the Supreme Leader and the citizens of Zembay through a referendum. Amendments must be publicly recorded and accessible to all citizens.
